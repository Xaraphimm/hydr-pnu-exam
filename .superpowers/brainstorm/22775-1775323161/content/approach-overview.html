How should the app be structured?

Three approaches for organizing 29 subtopics across Airframe & Powerplant

A

Two-Tab Split: Airframe | Powerplant

Homepage has two big tabs at the top. Each tab shows its subtopics as a scrollable list of cards. Tap a subtopic to enter its Study/Flashcards/Test experience. Simple, mirrors the real exam structure (AMA vs AMP).

Pros

  • Clean mental model — matches FAA exam categories
  • Easy to find your topic
  • Room for a progress dashboard per category

Cons

  • Can't see both categories at once
  • Two extra taps to reach a subtopic
B

Single Scrollable Homepage with Section Headers

One long page with "Airframe" and "Powerplant" as section headers. All 29 subtopics visible as cards in a single scroll. Each card shows a progress ring and question count. Tap to enter the subtopic.

Pros

  • Everything visible at once — no hidden content
  • One tap to reach any subtopic
  • Progress visible across all topics instantly

Cons

  • Long scroll with 29 cards
  • Could feel overwhelming on first visit
C

Collapsible Accordion: Airframe / Powerplant

Homepage shows two large expandable sections. Tap "Airframe" to expand and reveal its 15 subtopics. Tap "Powerplant" to expand its 14. Both can be open simultaneously. Compact when collapsed, detailed when expanded.

Pros

  • Compact by default, detailed on demand
  • Works well on mobile — focused view
  • Can show summary stats on the collapsed headers

Cons

  • Two taps to reach a subtopic (expand + tap)
  • Accordion patterns can feel clunky

My recommendation: Option B — the single scrollable homepage. On mobile, 29 cards is actually quite manageable with compact card designs, and it means everything is one tap away. The section headers provide the Airframe/Powerplant grouping without hiding content behind tabs or accordions. Progress rings on each card give you the dashboard for free.
